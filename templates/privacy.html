Privacy policy

Last updated June 2026 · Draft for photographer review

Short version: Your galleries are yours. We use them only to draft your album. We do not train models on your images. Each account sees only its own albums.

Who we are

mnemosyne is an AI album-design tool for professional photographers. This policy describes how we handle data when you use the waitlist, create an account, or upload a gallery.

What we collect

  • Account data — email and a hashed password for sign-in.
  • Waitlist — email address when you join early access.
  • Galleries — image files you upload, plus metadata we derive (dimensions, scene labels, layout choices) to build your album draft.
  • Usage metering — token counts and inference costs per album so we can price the service honestly (not the image bytes themselves).

How we use your photos

Uploaded images are processed to analyze each shot (scene + hero-worthiness) and to lay out spreads. That is the entire product. We do not sell your images, use them in marketing, or add them to a training dataset.

We do not train on your images

mnemosyne does not fine-tune or train machine-learning models on your uploads. In production, vision and layout run through a cloud inference API (currently xAI/Grok) under API terms that do not use customer inputs to train their models. We choose providers and configurations with that constraint in mind.

On local / dogfood setups, images may stay entirely on your own hardware and never leave your network.

Isolation & access

Every album belongs to one account. You cannot see another photographer's galleries, and they cannot see yours. Share links are optional, time-limited tokens you control — revoke or let them expire at any time.

Storage & retention

Photos are stored under your account until you delete the album. Deleting an album removes its database rows and stored image bytes. Backups, if any, follow the same retention window as production data.

Third parties

  • Cloud inference — receives downscaled image derivatives for analysis (not full-resolution marketing files unless you uploaded them).
  • Object storage — when enabled, encrypted at rest in our storage bucket (e.g. Cloudflare R2).
  • Print cross-sell — if you attach a Plutus offer link, clients who open your share page may follow that link to a separate print storefront.

Your choices

  • Delete albums (and their photos) from your account at any time.
  • Revoke share links before they expire.
  • Request account deletion by contacting us (feature coming; email for now).

Contact

Questions about this policy: use the email you joined the waitlist with, or reach out through the same channel we used to invite you to early access.
